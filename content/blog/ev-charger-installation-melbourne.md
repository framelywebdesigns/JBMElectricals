EV ownership is climbing fast across Melbourne, and home charging is becoming the standard. If you are looking at getting a charger installed, here is what you actually need to know.

## You do not need a fast charger at home

The most common mistake we see is people requesting commercial-grade fast chargers for home use. You do not need it. A standard 7kW home charger fully charges most EVs overnight. Fast chargers are for highway stops where you have 20 minutes, not for sitting in your garage for 10 hours.

A 7kW home charger is cheaper, easier to install, and does the job perfectly.

## What the install involves

Every home is different but most installs involve:

- A dedicated circuit run from your switchboard to where the charger sits
- A new breaker added to the switchboard
- The charger unit mounted on the wall (garage, carport, or external)
- Compliance testing and certification

Most jobs take half a day. The pricing depends on how far the cable run is and whether your switchboard has room for an extra circuit.

## Switchboard capacity matters

If your switchboard is old or already at capacity, you may need a small upgrade before the charger goes in. We always check this first so there are no surprises. Sometimes the switchboard work costs more than the charger install itself.

## Rebates worth knowing about

The Victorian government has been running incentive schemes for EV charger installation. These change from time to time so it is worth checking what is current when you book. We can point you towards what is available at the time of quote.
