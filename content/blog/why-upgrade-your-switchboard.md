If you live in an older home in Melbourne, chances are your switchboard has not been touched since the place was built. That is not necessarily a problem, but it is worth knowing what is sitting behind that little grey panel.

## Why old switchboards cause issues

Older homes were wired for a different time. Fewer appliances, no air conditioning, no induction cooktops, no EV chargers. The original switchboard was sized for that load. Today's homes draw far more power, and old boards struggle to keep up.

Common signs your switchboard might need an upgrade:

- Circuit breakers tripping more often than they used to
- Flickering lights when an appliance kicks in
- Ceramic fuses instead of modern circuit breakers
- No safety switches (RCDs) protecting the circuits
- Visible scorch marks or burn smells

## What an upgrade actually does

A switchboard upgrade is more than just swapping a panel. The work usually includes balancing the electrical load across phases, installing modern circuit breakers, adding safety switches for every circuit, and bringing the board up to current Australian Standards.

For most Melbourne homes the job takes a day. Power gets cut for a few hours while we work, and you get a written compliance certificate when we are done.

## When it pays for itself

If you are adding solar, an EV charger, or upgrading to ducted air conditioning, the switchboard upgrade often happens as part of that work anyway. Doing it proactively before something fails means you are not stuck without power on a 40 degree day waiting for an emergency callout.
